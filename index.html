GoalSync

The recent pandemic has forced people to become socially isolated from others and separated from their regular routines. With this habit-forming app, they have the capability, help, and motivation to adopt new habits that will continue to better their daily routines and overall lives in the future.



Team Members: Mia Steele Josh Merstein Dave Brewster

My Role: UX Researcher


Problem

It is difficult for people to incorporate new healthy habits into their daily life. We hope to help users form healthy habits using a new app that we create so they will be more susceptible to adopting and retaining formed habits.GoalSync is designed to help users form and stick to new habits through accountability. We have observed that current products aren’t meeting these goals.

Solution

As a team, we wanted to create an app so that users are successful in adopting and retaining healthy habits by giving them the ability to connect with other users that have common goals.

Inspiration

The recent pandemic has forced people to become socially isolated from others and separated from their normal routines. In this time of isolation, people will be able to self-reflect on their lifestyle choices and how they want to manage/change their physical and mental health routines right now and after this quarantine is over. People have been given a chance to slow down and with this habit-forming app, they have the capability, help, and motivation to adopt new habits that will continue to to better their daily routines and overall lives in the future.

Research

We conducted 9 user interviews on people that we believed fit our user proto-persona. Our main research objectives were to understand what motivates people to start healthy habits, acknowledge how people adopt healthy habits and recognize how people stick to adopted habits so we could gauge what users wanted in an app that would guide their good habits.

Synthesis

To synthesize our interviews our team created an Affinity Diagram. We found that most people felt that reward-based reinforcements kept them more focused on their goals and had trouble sticking to habits when they weren’t held accountable.

Analysis

We conducted a competitor analysis to gain insight into the strengths and weaknesses of the other popular and unpopular health apps. These competitors were successful in motivating people by creating a network of people with similar goals and had unique reward systems.

Prototyping/Testing

After We mapped out a user flow for our prototypes, each team member created prototype sketches.

Mid-fitesting

We conducted 6 user tests, two were remotely-moderated, four were in-person moderated tests. The most frequent problems from the low fidelity prototypes and was the category page, Scheduling page.

IOS-Mock up/HI-Fi

We created a final prototype with with Apple Human Interface Guideline. We based our final mock up on based on some of the final testing and to give direction for future development.



Conclusion

Any further steps our team would take would be to polish up the main homepage by adding more color and explanatory steps. During the last round of user testing, we found that most people needed an easier way to begin their journeys and maybe even add in a way to sync up to their other health-induced lifestyle tools.
